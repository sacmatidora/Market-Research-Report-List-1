<p><ul><li>Starch-based Fuel Ethanol</li><li>Sugar-based Fuel Ethanol</li><li>Cellulosic Fuel Ethanol</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The fuel ethanol market comprises three primary types: starch-based, sugar-based, and cellulosic fuel ethanol. Starch-based ethanol is derived from crops like corn and wheat, utilizing their starch content for fermentation. Sugar-based ethanol is produced from sugarcane or sugar beet, leveraging the sugars for conversion into fuel. Cellulosic ethanol, the most advanced type, is made from non-food plant materials, such as agricultural residues and wood, providing a sustainable alternative by reducing competition with food resources.</p></p>
