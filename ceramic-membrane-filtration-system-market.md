<p><p>The ceramic membrane filtration system market consists of three primary types: microfiltration, ultrafiltration, and nanofiltration. Microfiltration employs membranes with larger pore sizes to separate particles and microorganisms from liquids, making it effective for water treatment and industrial processes. Ultrafiltration, or hyperfiltration, utilizes smaller pores to remove smaller solutes and colloids, while nanofiltration features even finer membranes, selectively allowing certain ions and small molecules to pass while retaining larger organic compounds. Each type serves distinct applications across various industries.</p></p>
