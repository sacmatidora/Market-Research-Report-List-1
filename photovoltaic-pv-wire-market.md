<p><p>The Photovoltaic (PV) wire market comprises primarily two types of conductors: aluminum and copper. Aluminum conductors are lightweight, cost-effective, and resistant to corrosion, making them suitable for large-scale solar installations. However, they have higher resistance, which may lead to energy loss. Copper conductors, on the other hand, offer superior conductivity and durability, ensuring better energy efficiency but are generally more expensive. Both types play vital roles in connecting solar panels to inverters and ensuring optimal performance of solar energy systems.</p></p>
